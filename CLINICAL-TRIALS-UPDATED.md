## Key Trials for Affordable Prosthetics

### 1. Low Cost Prosthetic Socket for Lower Limb Amputees (NCT04725461)
- **Status**: ✅ COMPLETED (2018–2025)
- **Sponsor**: Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (Chicago, IL)
- **Innovation**: Expandable rigid foam socket fitting protocol — cast limb in <30 min, finish socket in 90 min
- **Key Insight**: Designed for developing countries; users cannot return for multiple fittings
- **Relevance**: Directly addresses affordable prosthetic access in low-resource settings
- **Link**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT04725461
- **Team Lead**: Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (Chicago team should lead engagement)

### 2. Reverse Innovation: ReMotion Prosthetic Knee (NCT04700085)
- **Status**: ✅ COMPLETED (2019)
- **Sponsor**: Sint Maartenskliniek (Netherlands)
- **Innovation**: $80 mechanical knee vs. $1,000–$4,000 standard knees
- **Key Insight**: Reverse innovation from low-income country design now evaluated for high-income use
- **Relevance**: Demonstrates cost-saving potential of reverse innovation in prosthetics
- **Link**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT04700085

### 3. ENABLE System for Socket Fit Assessment (NCT05425810)
- **Status**: 🔴 RECRUITING (estimated completion Feb 2026)
- **Sponsor**: North Carolina State University
- **Innovation**: Low-cost pressure-sensing pad using thermal-sensitive dyes and gallium-alloy nanotubes
- **Key Insight**: Visual socket fit assessment without expensive equipment — ideal for resource-limited settings
- **Relevance**: Directly applicable to outreach; could reduce fitting errors and improve comfort
- **Link**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05425810
- **Action**: Outreach coordinators should monitor for results and potential collaboration
- **Team Lead**: Boston team (academic collaboration + monitoring)

### 4. AI-Powered 3D-Printed Prosthetic Hand (NCT07437664)
- **Status**: ⏳ ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING (Al-Nahrain University, Baghdad, Iraq)
- **Innovation**: AI + 3D-printed components for transradial amputees; adaptive grasping via EMG
- **Key Insight**: Full 3D-printed prosthetics with AI control at minimal cost
- **Relevance**: Shows how AI + additive manufacturing can produce affordable upper-limb prosthetics
- **Link**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07437664
- **Action**: High-priority for technical reference

### 5. 3D-Printed Prosthetic Foot - PROFIL (NCT06938087)
- **Status**: ✅ COMPLETED (2025)
- **Sponsor**: Istituto Nazionale Assicurazione contro gli Infortuni sul Lavoro (Italy)
- **Innovation**: Energy-storing and return (ESAR) foot made via additive manufacturing with continuous carbon filament
- **Key Insight**: 3D printing can reduce costs while maintaining or improving performance vs. commercial carbon fiber designs
- **Relevance**: Additive manufacturing as a cost-reduction strategy for prosthetic feet
- **Link**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06938087

### 6. VR Rehabilitation for Amputees (NCT06424249)
- **Status**: 🔴 RECRUITING
- **Sponsor**: Centre Hospitalier Universitaire de Nîmes (France)
- **Innovation**: Virtual reality for dynamic balance training in transtibial amputees
- **Key Insight**: Personalized rehabilitation with objective progress tracking
- **Relevance**: Affordable rehabilitation approach; could reduce in-person PT costs
- **Link**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06424249
