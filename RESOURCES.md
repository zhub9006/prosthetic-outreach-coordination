# Resources & Clinical Trials

## Key Clinical Trials on Affordable Prosthetics

### 1. Low Cost Prosthetic Socket for Lower Limb Amputees (NCT04725461)
- **Sponsor**: Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (Chicago, IL)
- **Status**: COMPLETED (2018–2025)
- **Focus**: Affordable socket fabrication using expandable rigid foam for transtibial amputees in developing countries
- **Innovation**: Simplified socket fitting protocol — cast limb in <30 min, finish socket in 90 min using expandable polyurethane foam
- **Relevance**: Directly addresses affordable prosthetic access in low-resource settings; designed for settings where users cannot return for multiple fittings
- **PI**: Laura Miller
- **Location**: Chicago, IL
- **URL**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT04725461

### 2. Reverse Innovation: ReMotion Prosthetic Knee (NCT04700085)
- **Sponsor**: Sint Maartenskliniek (Netherlands)
- **Status**: COMPLETED (2019)
- **Focus**: Evaluating the $80 ReMotion Knee as a reverse innovation for affordable prosthetic care
- **Innovation**: Low-cost mechanical knee designed for low-income countries, now being evaluated for high-income country use — $80 vs. $1,000–$4,000 standard mechanical knees
- **Relevance**: Demonstrates cost-saving potential of reverse innovation in prosthetics across all income levels; could reduce healthcare costs dramatically
- **PI**: Noël LW Keijsers, PhD
- **Location**: Ubbergen, Netherlands
- **URL**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT04700085

### 3. ENABLE System for Socket Fit Assessment (NCT05425810)
- **Sponsor**: North Carolina State University (Raleigh, NC)
- **Status**: RECRUITING (estimated completion Feb 2026)
- **Focus**: Affordable pressure-sensing pad (ENABLE) for visual socket fit assessment using thermal-sensitive dyes and nanotube technology
- **Innovation**: Low-cost sensor system integrated into prosthetic liners to help prosthetists evaluate socket fit without expensive equipment — uses gallium-alloy nanotubes and thermal dyes to visualize pressure distribution
- **Relevance**: Addresses challenge of accurate socket fitting in resource-limited settings; could reduce fitting errors and improve comfort for amputees
- **PI**: Ming Liu, PhD
- **Location**: Raleigh, NC
- **URL**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05425810

### 4. AI-Powered 3D-Printed Prosthetic Hand (NCT07437664)
- **Sponsor**: Al-Nahrain University (Baghdad, Iraq)
- **Status**: ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING
- **Focus**: Low-cost AI-powered robotic hand with 3D-printed sockets for transradial amputees
- **Innovation**: Uses AI to classify EMG signals for adaptive grasping; fully 3D-printed components reduce manufacturing cost significantly
- **Relevance**: Demonstrates how AI + additive manufacturing can produce affordable upper-limb prosthetics for resource-limited settings
- **PI**: Wajdi Sadik Aboud, Prof. Dr.
- **Location**: Baghdad, Iraq
- **URL**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07437664

### 5. 3D-Printed Prosthetic Foot (PROFIL) (NCT06938087)
- **Sponsor**: Istituto Nazionale Assicurazione contro gli Infortuni sul Lavoro (Italy)
- **Status**: COMPLETED (2025)
- **Focus**: Energy-storing and return (ESAR) prosthetic foot made via additive manufacturing
- **Innovation**: Continuous filament carbon 3D printing enables customized, cost-effective prosthetic feet with dynamic energy return comparable to commercial carbon fiber designs
- **Relevance**: Additive manufacturing can substantially reduce prosthetic foot costs while maintaining or improving performance
- **PI**: Emanuele Gruppioni
- **Location**: Rome, Italy
- **URL**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06938087

## Prosthetics Trend Analysis
Based on ClinicalTrials.gov data for prosthetic studies:
- **Total studies**: ~2,182
- **Phase distribution**: Most are Phase N/A (early/observational) — indicating a young field with many feasibility studies
- **Sponsor types**: Primarily industry-sponsored (~382), with significant academic/government involvement
- **Growing area**: 3D printing and additive manufacturing for prosthetics is a rapidly expanding field
- **Affordability focus**: Emerging from low-income country innovations (ReMotion Knee, low-cost sockets) now being evaluated in high-income settings as cost-saving reverse innovations
